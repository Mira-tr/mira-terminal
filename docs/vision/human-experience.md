# Human Experience

RELMUA uses Human Experience, or HX, as a quality standard.

UX can sound like screen design.

HX means the whole experience a person has with RELMUA:

- What they understand.
- What they fear.
- What they can recover from.
- What they know to do next.
- Whether they can return later without relearning everything.

## HX Standard

RELMUA should be usable by a beginner in five minutes.

It should also remain understandable to a future maintainer after five years.

## Target People

### A First-Time Middle School Student

They should not need to know:

- JSON.
- Build.
- Manifest.
- Git.
- Folder structure.

They should understand:

- What they can add.
- What they can edit.
- Whether it is safe.
- What happens when they press a button.
- How to go back.

### Creator

A Creator should understand:

- Where their site is.
- Where their works are.
- Where their profile is.
- Which collections belong to them.
- What is public and what is private.

Creator experience must not be swallowed by Brand or by one module.

### Administrator

An administrator should understand:

- Project health.
- Build status.
- Validation status.
- Backup recency.
- Publish readiness.
- What needs action today.

The admin experience should be dense enough for daily work, but not cryptic.

### Collaborator

A collaborator should understand:

- Where data lives conceptually.
- Which module or creator owns a change.
- What review is needed.
- What risks exist before publish.
- What decision record explains the design.

### Future Self After Five Years

Future maintainers should understand:

- Why the architecture exists.
- What is safe to change.
- Which data is canonical.
- How migration works.
- How to restore from mistakes.

## HX Rules

- Do not expose complexity before it is needed.
- Explain technical actions in human language.
- Show one next action.
- Make recovery visible.
- Keep Public as a calm visitor experience.
- Keep Studio as a safe production desk.
- Keep Creator Sites independent and understandable.

## HX Test

For every important screen, ask:

- Can a beginner explain what this page is for?
- Can they predict what the main button does?
- Can they find their way back?
- Can they tell whether data is saved?
- Can they tell whether it is safe to publish?
- Can a future maintainer understand why this exists?
